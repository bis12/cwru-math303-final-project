Mersenne Primes

Tom Dooner, Brian Stack

Mersenne, And His Numbers

  • Marin Mersenne (1588-1648) was a prolific mathematician and music theorist.
  • Mersenne Numbers are of the form $$M_p = 2^{n} - 1$$
  • Mersenne conjectured that \(M_p\) is prime for \(n=2, 3, 5, 7, 13, 17, 19, 31, 67, 127, 257\) (\(0 < n \leq 257)\)
  • The Mersenne Numbers that are prime are called Mersenne Primes.

Properties of Mersenne Primes

  • \(2^n-1\) is prime implies that \(n\) is prime!
  • Lenstra-Pomerance-Wagstaff Conjecture: There are infinitely many Mersenne Primes?
  • There are fourty-seven known Mersenne Primes.
  • For \(n\leq257\), \(2^n-1\) is prime for \(n=2, 3, 5, 7, 13, 17, 19, 31, \textbf{61}, \textbf{89}, \textbf{107}, 127\). (67 and 257 are composite)
